Digital Dreamscapes: A Net Art Exhibition

Curated by Cindy Nguyen

Curatorial Statement

With all the different types of websites that can be used to express a person’s self-identity, or what they like, I’ve decided to choose these 6 websites! As you look through each one, it could inspire you to create your own for feature references. These six websites show a variety of do-it-yourself mentality, self-portraits, or a community of artists. Most importantly, all 6 of these websites show some type of cyber digital design to them, whether it be the images used, the fonts, or the whole website as a whole.
You’ll first see how two websites on the left hand columns (Elle’s, ToothLilli's) are autobiographies of themselves. They include their interests, as well as their projects for everyone to explore. Next you have the middle column (GoblinHeart, MelonLand), which are websites for discovery. Showing off different artists and their talents. Finally you have the two in the right columns (Cameron’s, Tetrageddon), sites that display a more interactive portion. As you click through the sites, you're able to see different sights they display.

Featured Net Artworks

Elle’s Homepage (2025) by Elle

Visit Elle's Homepage

A personal homepage with sections describing Elle's hobbies and projects, as well as links to her profiles on sites like Bluesky, Itch.io, and Tumblr, it acts as a central location for her online persona.

Goblin-Heart.net (2024) by SadGrl

Visit Goblin-Heart.net

An interactive personal website that has blog, library, and themed rooms are among the areas that visitors can explore after being welcomed by graphical art.

Cameron's World (2015) by Cameron Askin

Visit Cameron's World

A tribute to the early days of personal web expression and the raw creativity of that era, this web collage is made up of text and images from archived GeoCities pages (1994–2009).

ToothLilli's Pillowfort (2025) by ToothLilli

Visit ToothLilli's Pillowfort

A website that serves as the primary point of ToothLilli's internet activity, it has links to her profiles on many websites, including Bluesky, Itch.io, and Tumblr, as well as parts exhibiting her artwork, essays, and projects.

MelonLand (2024) by Melon aka Daniel

Visit MelonLand

A web project and online arts community celebrating the disordered appeal of the early web, virtual worlds, and personal homepages.

Tetrageddon (2020) by Nathalie Lawhead

Visit Tetrageddon

Offering a strange and entertaining experience reminiscent of the anarchic charm of the early web, this interactive art project and web game collection embraces the aesthetics of early internet culture.
